WF8 · Functional Specification · March 2026
Outbound Campaigns — Deep Dive
Human-approved batch email sends. Every step from campaign dispatch through approval gate to execution and follow-up.
Section 1
Three Campaign Types
Every campaign requires explicit human approval via Slack before any email sends. No exceptions.
Type 1
Announcement
What: Company news, capability launch, case study.
Audience: Warm contacts — Discovery+ deals, leads with engagement.
Trigger: Manual dispatch from Ben/Emmet.
Templates:
announcement-enterprise.md
announcement-mid-market.md
Type 2
Cold Outreach
What: Industry-specific outreach to enriched, uncontacted contacts.
Audience: No deals, no recent engagement, scored.
Trigger: Manual dispatch OR Monday auto-batch.
Templates:
cold-cpg.md cold-automotive.md cold-beauty.md
Type 3
Follow-Up (No-Reply)
What: Single follow-up to non-responders, 5+ biz days after send.
Audience: Prior WF8 recipients with no reply.
Trigger: Auto-detected in WF6 sweep. Drafted, never auto-sent.
Template: follow-up-no-reply.md
Rule: ONE follow-up per contact per campaign.
Section 2
10-Step Batch Assembly
Campaign instruction → assemble → personalize → approve → send. The approval gate at Step 9 is the human control point.
1
Receive Campaign Instruction Human Trigger
Manual: Ben/Emmet DMs REV or posts to #klaw-rev-input.
Scheduled: Monday after weekly report → auto-generates cold batch for contacts uncontacted 60+ days.
2
Parse Campaign Intent
Extract: campaign type (announcement/cold/follow-up), target segment (industry, lifecycle, tier), template family, personalization signals to emphasize.
3
Query HubSpot
Search contacts by industry, lead_tier, lifecyclestage, hs_email_last_send_date. Must have kartel_score. Pull name, email, title, company, score, tier, industry.
4
Apply Exclusion Filters 6 Checks
Remove any contact matching these — breakdown logged for transparency:
① lifecyclestage = customer (existing clients)
② unsubscribed = true (CAN-SPAM)
③ hs_email_last_send_date within 30 days
④ sdr_sequence_status = active (in WF7 sequence)
⑤ email domain = @kartel.ai (internal)
⑥ Deal in Negotiation or Procurement (AE handles)
5
Enrich Unenriched Mandatory
Missing Clay data → find-and-enrich-list-of-contacts via Clay MCP. Unenriched contacts excluded from batch (Safeguard #2). If Clay unavailable: remove them, don't hold batch.
6
Score Unscored
Contacts without kartel_score → trigger WF4. Score used for template selection and AE assignment.
7
Select Template Per Contact
Announcement: Enterprise → announcement-enterprise · Other → announcement-mid-market
Cold: CPG → cold-cpg · Auto → cold-automotive · Beauty → cold-beauty · Other → cold-cpg (default)
Follow-up: Any → follow-up-no-reply
8
Personalize Each Email
Fill template variables. Clay signal priority for {{clay_signal}}:
① Recent funding round (most compelling)
② Headcount growth >20%
③ Recent news / press
④ Open relevant roles (production, creative, marketing)
⑤ Industry trend (generic fallback)
9
Approval Gate Human Required
Full batch table posted to #klaw-rev-output: every contact with name, company, title, score, tier, Clay signal, template. Exclusion breakdown included.
SEND → execute all · HOLD → individual contact cards · SEND 1,3,5 → selective · CANCEL → discard
10
Execute on Approval Automated
Send from sdr@kartel.ai via Gmail API. 10/hr, 60s gap, daily cap. Each send: HubSpot engagement logged, hs_email_last_send_date updated, engagement_score +5.
Section 3
Approval Gate — Four Response Paths
Ben or Emmet's Slack reply determines execution. Each path has a defined outcome.
"SEND"
Execute Full Batch
All emails sent. Each logged to HubSpot with full content. hs_email_last_send_date updated. engagement_score +5. Completion posted to #klaw-rev-output.
"HOLD"
Individual Review
One card per contact posted to #klaw-rev-output with email preview and Clay signals. Reply SEND {id} or SKIP {id} per contact.
"SEND 1,3,5"
Selective Send
Only specified contacts sent. Others discarded (not held). Can be re-included in future campaigns.
"CANCEL"
Discard Batch
Entire batch dropped. Logged: "Campaign canceled by {approver}." Contacts remain eligible for future batches.
Section 4
Follow-Up Automation — 5-Day No-Reply
5 biz days after a campaign, REV auto-detects non-responders and drafts a follow-up batch. Always requires SEND confirmation. Never auto-sends.
1
Detect Non-Responders
During WF6 morning sweep: query contacts with hs_email_last_send_date 5+ biz days ago, no Gmail reply, unsubscribed = false.
2
Filter Converted
Remove anyone who replied, booked a meeting, or was contacted by an AE since the original campaign.
3
Assemble Follow-Up Batch
Use follow-up-no-reply.md template. Original campaign context + "following up" framing. 80 words max.
4
Post for Approval Same Step 9 Format
Standard batch table. SEND / HOLD / CANCEL. One follow-up per contact per campaign — after this, contact returns to monitor pool.
ONE follow-up per contact per campaign. No reply after follow-up → monitor pool. No further auto-outreach.
Monday auto-batch is a convenience — it STILL requires human approval.
Section 5
Reply Monitoring & WF6 Integration
WF8 has no dedicated reply loop or cron step. It hooks into existing WF6 infrastructure.
Reply Detection (WF6 Step 1)
Replies to outbound emails detected on sdr@kartel.ai during WF6 Gmail scans.

Interested: Post to #klaw-rev-output. No active deal → flag WF1.
Unsubscribe: Set unsubscribed = true. Logged.
OOO: Note return date. Include in next campaign after return.
WF6 Integration Points
Dispatch: Event-driven (Slack), not sweep-based.
Monday batch: After Monday weekly report (end of WF6 Step 9).
Follow-up detection: Morning sweep alongside WF7 Phase D.
Reply detection: WF6 Step 1 Gmail scan.
Execution: Morning sweep after approval. Morning-only sends.

Section 7
Compliance & Critical Pitfalls
Compliance — Non-Negotiable
1
CAN-SPAM: Unsubscribe link in every template. Opt-outs processed within 24hrs.
2
Opt-out: unsubscribed = true → excluded from all future batches immediately.
3
30-day cooldown: Contacts emailed via WF8 excluded from new batches for 30 days.
4
Client protection: lifecyclestage = customer → NEVER in any outbound batch.
5
Active deal protection: Negotiation/Procurement contacts excluded.
6
Identity: sdr@kartel.ai isolated from team inboxes.
Deliverability Caps (Shared WF7 + WF8)
Daily cap (warm-up)10 → 20 → 30
Daily cap (post warm-up)50
Per-hour cap10
Min gap between sends60 seconds
Bounce rate threshold3% → pause + alert
Spam complaint rate0.1% → pause + escalate Niels
Priority: WF7 inbound sends before WF8 outbound if cap is approaching.
12 Rules That Must Never Break
1
Never send without explicit SEND from Ben or Emmet
2
Never include existing customers (lifecyclestage = customer)
3
Never include unsubscribed contacts
4
Never re-contact within 30 days
5
Never exceed daily cap (WF7 has priority)
6
Never send during midday/evening — morning only
7
Never send >1 follow-up per contact per campaign
8
Always enrich before including in batch (Safeguard #2)
9
Always log every send as HubSpot engagement with full content
10
Always include unsubscribe mechanism (CAN-SPAM)
11
Always show exclusion breakdown in batch summary
12
Monday auto-batch still requires human approval
